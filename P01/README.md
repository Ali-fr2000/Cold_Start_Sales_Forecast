## 📋 Overview

This repository contains a clustering-based machine learning pipeline designed to solve the **"cold-start" forecasting problem** in retail and B2B e-commerce. When new products are launched, they lack historical sales data, making traditional time-series forecasting methods ineffective.

This pipeline bypasses that limitation by:
- Aggregating early-stage sales data from historical products
- Clustering products based on their launch-phase behavior (first 8 weeks)
- Training a classifier to map newly launched products to similar historical clusters
- Generating probabilistic 8-week sales forecasts for new product launches

**Context:** This project was developed as part of an MSc Thesis research initiative focusing on Supply Chain Analytics and Demand Forecasting.

---

## 🧠 Methodology

The pipeline operates on a robust two-step approach:

### Step 1: Unsupervised Clustering (Historical Data)
Historical products are clustered using K-Means based on their sales behavior **strictly during their initial launch phase** (first 8 weeks only), ignoring later performance to focus on "cold-start" patterns.

### Step 2: Supervised Classification (New Products)
A Logistic Regression model is trained to predict a product's cluster using only **static, launch-time features** such as:
- Category and sub-category hierarchy
- Brand novelty and market presence
- Price positioning
- Competitive landscape

### The Forecast
New products are classified into a cluster upon launch. Their 8-week sales forecast is generated by computing the historical mean of the 8-week total sales for all products belonging to the assigned cluster.

---

## ✨ Key Features

- **Strict Temporal Splitting:** Chronological division of Train, Validation, and Test sets based on the `first_sale` timestamp to prevent future data leakage
- **Inflation-Adjusted Price Indices:** Automatically compounds regional inflation rates across years to normalize product pricing
- **Granular Substitutability:** Dynamically calculates `num_similars` by counting active predecessor products within the exact category hierarchy
- **Parallelized k-Sweep Optimization:** Distributes K-Means and Logistic Regression training across available CPU cores using `joblib`
- **Comprehensive Output:** Exports both macro-level performance metrics (WMAPE, RMSE, MAE, Bias) and detailed product-level forecasts to Excel

## 📊 Evaluation Metrics

The pipeline evaluates forecast accuracy using:

- **WMAPE (Weighted Mean Absolute Percentage Error)** — Primary metric, robust to high variance and intermittent sales
- **RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)** — Penalizes larger deviations
- **MAE (Mean Absolute Error)** — Average absolute deviation
- **Bias** — Systematic over/under-forecasting tendency

These metrics are computed at both macro (aggregated) and micro (product-level) granularity.

## 🚧 Known Limitations & Future Work

### Current Limitations
- **Bias Analysis:** Further investigation into systemic over/under-forecasting biases across specific sub-categories is required
- **Unknown Features Handling:** Currently, unseen categorical features (brands, producers) in validation/test sets are handled by `OneHotEncoder(handle_unknown='ignore')`. More robust imputation strategies are planned
- **Similar Products Logic:** The `num_similars` feature counts historical predecessors but does not verify if those predecessors were *still in stock/active* at the time of the new product launch

### Planned Improvements
- Converting raw `num_similars` counts into categorical bins (Low, Medium, High)
- Developing semantic product substitute definitions beyond rigid category hierarchies
- Exploring advanced clustering algorithms (DBSCAN, Gaussian Mixtures)
- Implementing confidence intervals and uncertainty quantification
- Adding support for external features (seasonality, marketing campaigns, etc.)
