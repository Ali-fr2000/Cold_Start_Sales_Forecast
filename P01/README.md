## Overview
This repository contains a clustering-based machine learning pipeline designed to solve the **"cold-start" forecasting problem** in retail and B2B e-commerce. When new products are launched, they lack historical sales data, making traditional time-series forecasting ineffective. 

This pipeline bypasses that limitation by aggregating early-stage sales, clustering products based on their historical launch behavior, and training a classifier to map newly launched products to these behavioral clusters using only pre-launch features.

This project was developed as part of an MSc Thesis research initiative focusing on Supply Chain Analytics and Demand Forecasting.

---

## 🧠 Methodology

The pipeline operates on a robust two-step heuristic:
1. **Unsupervised Clustering (Historical Data):** Historical products are clustered (using K-Means) based on their sales behavior strictly during their initial launch phase (first 8 weeks), ignoring long-term steady-state behaviors.
2. **Supervised Classification (New Products):** A Logistic Regression model is trained to predict a product's cluster using *only* static, launch-time features (e.g., category, brand novelty, price index, substitutability). 

**The Forecast:** New products are classified into a cluster upon launch. Their 8-week sales forecast is generated by taking the historical mean of the 8-week total sales for all products belonging to that predicted cluster.

---

## ✨ Key Features

* **Strict Temporal Splitting:** Chronological division of Train, Validation, and Test sets based on the `first_sale` timestamp to strictly prevent future data leakage.
* **Inflation-Adjusted Price Indices:** Automatically compounds regional inflation rates across years to normalize product pricing over long time horizons.
* **Granular Substitutability:** Calculates `num_similars` by dynamically counting active predecessor products within the exact `Category - sub_cat - sub_sub_cat` path.
* **Parallelized $k$-Sweep Optimization:** Distributes K-Means and Logistic Regression training across all available CPU cores using `joblib` to find the optimal number of clusters, favoring model simplicity (lower $k$) for marginal WMAPE gains.
* **Granular Output:** Exports both macro-level performance metrics (WMAPE, RMSE, MAE, Bias) and detailed, product-level forecasts to Excel.

## 📊 Evaluation Metrics

The pipeline evaluates forecast accuracy primarily through **WMAPE (Weighted Mean Absolute Percentage Error)** to handle the high variance and intermittent nature of new product sales, alongside standard RMSE, MAE, and Bias metrics.

---

## 🚧 Known Limitations & Future Work

* **Bias Analysis:** Further investigation into systemic over/under-forecasting biases across specific sub-categories is required.
* **Unknown Features Handling:** Currently, unseen categorical features (brands, producers) in the validation/test sets are caught by the `OneHotEncoder(handle_unknown='ignore')` parameter. Future iterations may benefit from target encoding or embedding techniques.
* **Similar Products Logic:** The `num_similars` feature counts historical predecessors but does not actively check if those predecessors were *still in stock/active* at the time of the new product's launch.
* **Feature Refinements:**
* Converting raw `num_similars` counts into generalized categorical bins (Low, Medium, High).
* Developing semantic product substitute definitions beyond the rigid `sub-sub-cat` hierarchy.
